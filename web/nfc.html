Frequently asked questions

Does the NFC tag track me or share data with anyone?
No. NFC tags are passive — no GPS, no battery, no microphone. They are silent until you physically tap them. Same privacy model as a phone-number sticker. Verified by Wikipedia and the NFC Forum.
What if a guest taps my tag — will they see my address?
No. They reach a Servia booking page that shows only the masked last-4-digits of the linked phone number. Your name, full phone and address are hidden until the page sees a sign-in token belonging to you (the owner).
Which phones work with NFC tap?
iPhone XS (2018) and newer · most Android phones since 2018 · all Samsung Galaxy S6+ · all Pixel and Pixel Watch · all Galaxy Watch 4+. NFC is on by default. No app required — your browser opens automatically.
What's the difference between NFC and a QR code?
QR = open camera, frame, focus, tap notification — 4 steps and 5–8 seconds. NFC = tap once, ~2 seconds. NFC works in the dark, in tight spaces, and you can hide it behind a cabinet door where a camera can't see it. QR is great for printed posters; NFC wins for repeat actions.
Can I have multiple NFC tags?
Yes — that's actually the recommended setup. One per service: kitchen tag for maid, AC tag for AC service, car tag for car wash, pool tag for pool care, etc. Each is bound to your same account but to a different service.
What if I lose a tag?
Sign in to your Servia account → My tags → Deactivate. Whoever finds it can't use it. We'll send you a replacement free.
Is this an open standard? Can I overwrite the tag?
Yes — Servia tags are standard NTAG213 chips (the NFC industry default). We pre-write a single URL to it — you can read or rewrite it any time with a free app like NFC Tools. We never lock the tag.
How long does a tag last?
NTAG213 chips are rated for 100,000 writes and ~10 years of read cycles. The plastic / paper substrate fails first; sticker version typically survives 3–5 years on a kitchen cabinet, longer in a wallet (card) or keychain.
