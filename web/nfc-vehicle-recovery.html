🔒 Important: How location actually works

The NFC tag is passive — no battery, no GPS, no broadcast. It does not know where it is or where your car is. When you tap your phone to the tag, your phone's browser opens Servia and (with your permission) shares your phone's GPS for that one dispatch only. The tag is never connected to anything. Same privacy model as a phone-number sticker on a fridge.

FAQ

How fast does Servia recovery arrive?
Average 18-24 min in Dubai/Sharjah/Ajman, 30-45 min in Abu Dhabi suburbs and northern emirates. Driver name, photo, truck plate and live ETA on WhatsApp the moment dispatch confirms.
Does the NFC tag track my car?
No. NFC is passive — no battery, no GPS, no broadcast. Location is only captured when you tap (using YOUR phone's GPS) and only sent to Servia's dispatch.
What if my phone is dead?
The tag itself works at 1% phone battery. If your phone is fully dead, anyone can tap their phone — we'll route recovery to that GPS and contact you via WhatsApp once available.
Which phones support this?
Every iPhone since XS (2018) and Android since 2018. NFC is on by default. More on phone compatibility.
How is this different from AAA / regular roadside?
Traditional services: dial → enter card details → describe location verbally → wait. Servia: 1 tap. GPS auto-captured. WhatsApp tracking. Compare NFC vs QR vs phone-call.
