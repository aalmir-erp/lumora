The 60-second pitch

What we are, in one paragraph

Servia is a home-services marketplace for the UAE — cleaning, plumbing, electrical, pest control, AC, handyman, NFC re-ordering, Airbnb turnover automation. We're not "another cleaning company with an app." We're the only player in the UAE category that combines: 7 production AI agents (customer service, vendor onboarding, content automation, dispatch, escalation, recovery, voice), an 8-provider LLM router with per-task model selection, a customer-owned NFC re-booking product, native Wear OS support, real-time RLAIF feedback loops, and Airbnb / Vrbo iCal calendar sync that auto-schedules turnover cleanings between every guest checkout and check-in. Every claim on this page is verifiable in our production codebase.

Honest framing: "Agentic AI" is the accurate term for what we do — orchestrating frontier LLMs (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini, DeepSeek, others) with tool-use, RAG, and real-world side effects. We do not train our own models; we route, orchestrate, and continuously tune on customer feedback. Anyone telling you they "train their own AI" for a home-services app is either burning $5M+ in compute for marginal gain, or misrepresenting. We're built on the right architecture for this market.

The honest risks

What could go wrong, and how we mitigate

Every investor deck shows risks. Most do it dishonestly — boilerplate "regulatory risk · key person risk" without any teeth. Below are the risks that genuinely keep me up at night, with the actual mitigation we run.

1. Incumbents wake up and copy the tech

Justlife with $30M raised could decide to build agentic AI tomorrow. Our 18-month head start collapses if they hire fast.

Mitigation: Data moat compounds — every RLAIF-rated chat makes our prompts harder to copy. NFC + Wear OS are physical/hardware moats that take quarters to ship. We move on Series A before incumbents finish their feasibility studies.

2. Anthropic / OpenAI pricing changes

If Claude pricing doubles, our gross margins compress. We're API-dependent.

Mitigation: 8-provider router already cascades across Claude/GPT/Gemini/Mistral/DeepSeek (DeepSeek V3 at 1/10th the cost). Fine-tuned UAE-LLM fallback (Q2 2027) further insulates us. We're never single-provider locked.

3. Customer trust on AI-handled bookings

If a Claude reply gets a price wrong by AED 200, the customer's first call is to Twitter. Brand damage compounds.

Mitigation: RLAIF loop flags low-scoring replies for admin review before the bad pattern repeats. AI-critic scoring is independent of customer reporting — we catch issues proactively. Every high-stakes action (booking confirm, payment) has a human-loop gate.

4. UAE crew supply for Airbnb turnover surge

If 500 Airbnb hosts sync calendars simultaneously, we need 50+ crews per day in the right neighbourhoods. Crew supply is the binding constraint.

Mitigation: Recovery + reverse-auction dispatch already handles supply-demand matching. Vendor-onboarding agent automates new crew acquisition. We can throttle iCal sync to match supply during the ramp.

5. NFC hardware logistics + cost

Shipping 10,000 NFC stickers/cards/keychains globally requires inventory + fulfillment we haven't fully built yet.

Mitigation: NFC tags are AED 8-15 per unit at scale (verified with 3 UAE suppliers). We ship from Dubai warehouse only. Fulfillment is a known-cost operations problem, not a tech problem.

6. Key-person risk (founder + AI orchestration)

If the founder is unavailable, the AI orchestration layer needs a senior eng who can maintain it.

Mitigation: First post-funding hire is a Staff Engineer for AI infra. Documentation + system runbooks already exist. Codebase is conventional FastAPI — not exotic patterns.
